Module 1 · PACE: Purpose
Set the Stage — AI Governance & Visual Assets
⏱ 0:05 – 0:27 · 22 min P — Purpose 🎤 Facilitator A
📋 AI Governance Context — Facilitator Note

A foundational principle of responsible AI use in any organization is working within your established AI governance framework and the technology infrastructure your organization provides. How we use AI is shaped by what our organization has approved, vetted for security, and made available to us — and that is a feature, not a limitation.

Today we are working with Microsoft CoPilot because it is the AI tool available within our current organizational environment. This is a deliberate, governance-aligned choice. Using the tools your organization sanctions means your work is compliant, secure, and scalable — and it models exactly the kind of responsible AI practice we are here to discuss.

As you facilitate this module, use this framing to set the tone: the question is not "which AI tool is best in the world" — it is "which AI tool is right for my context, and am I using it responsibly within my organization's guidelines?"

Learning Objectives
1
Identify at least two organizational AI governance principles that apply to learning asset creation.
2
Write a purposeful, specific prompt in Microsoft CoPilot and generate a visual asset concept.
3
Connect prompt quality to output quality using the "what you put in is what you get out" principle.
Content Delivery
10 min
🎤 Facilitator A — Script Notes

Open with energy and a grounding question to the room (in-person) and chat (online). Frame this as the "why before the how."

💬 Opening Prompt to Room

"Before we dive into the tools — raise your hand or drop it in the chat: how many of you have already used AI for something at work in the last 30 days? Great. And how many of you had a governance policy guiding that use?" [Pause for responses.] "That gap is exactly where we're starting today."

Talking Points — AI Governance

1
What is AI governance in L&D?
Define it simply: the organizational and ethical guardrails that guide how, when, and for what we use AI. This includes data privacy, copyright, accuracy, transparency with learners, and bias awareness.
2
Why governance matters for our work
AI doesn't inherently know your organization's values, brand voice, or sensitive topics. We are the guardrail. A governance mindset means we use AI as a collaborator, not a replacement decision-maker.
3
Responsible AI use checklist (quick 3-point)
(a) Never input confidential or PII data into AI tools. (b) Always disclose AI-assisted content when your org policy requires it. (c) You are responsible for the final output — AI is a tool, you are the author.
4
Introducing Microsoft CoPilot — your AI collaborator today
Brief orientation: Microsoft CoPilot, integrated into Microsoft 365. What it does well for L&D — content drafting, script writing, document creation, design briefs. Today we're using it alongside Microsoft 365 tools (Word, PowerPoint) to build visual and written learning assets.
⚡ Energy Cue — Transition to Prompt Engineering

"Now that we know WHY governance matters, let's talk about the thing that will make or break your AI results: your prompt. Think of CoPilot like a really smart new hire — the more specific your brief, the better the work you get back."

Talking Points — Prompt Engineering Foundations

5
What you put in is what you get out
Vague prompt = vague output. "Write me a training activity" vs "Write a 5-minute warm-up activity for a virtual onboarding session for new healthcare employees, age 25–40, focused on safety culture." Show both side-by-side if possible.
6
The four elements of a strong prompt
Role (what expertise CoPilot should apply) + Task (what you want) + Context (audience, constraints) + Format (how you want it delivered). Acronym: RTCF.
7
Different prompts for different assets
A prompt for an image concept looks different from a prompt for a facilitator guide. Today we'll practice both. The key is adapting your RTCF to the asset type.
Differentiated Support

Talking Points — AI Integration (CoPilot + Microsoft 365)

1
How CoPilot and Microsoft 365 work together
CoPilot is your thinking and writing engine. Microsoft 365 (Word, PowerPoint) is your design and publishing engine. The handoff: CoPilot generates structured content (a script, an outline, a design brief) → you bring that into Word or PowerPoint → Microsoft 365 formats, visualizes, and makes it production-ready.
2
Why using a framework matters
Without a framework, AI-assisted design becomes random. PACE gives us a consistent structure. ADDIE/SAM logic still applies — AI accelerates the steps, it doesn't replace them. Think of AI as a turbo button on your existing process.
3
Prompt specificity for written assets
Written assets need more context than visual ones. For a facilitator guide: specify timing, audience, tone, learning objectives, and format. For a video script: specify duration, speaker role, scene description, call to action. For a podcast intro: specify host voice, episode topic, hook style.
4
Prompt iteration is not failure — it's design
Your first prompt output is a first draft. Expert AI users iterate 2–5 times. Each iteration is a design decision. "Add a more conversational tone." "Shorten by 30%." "Make the opening line a question." This is part of the workflow, not a sign something went wrong.
⚡ Live Demo Cue (Optional — 2 min)

Open CoPilot and share your screen. Paste a prompt for a video script scene. Show the output live. Then say: "Watch what happens when I add one more line of context." Add "The tone should be warm, direct, and accessible for first-time managers" and regenerate. Discuss the difference.

Talking Points — Human Review & Workflow Integration

1
Human review is non-negotiable
AI does not know your learners, your culture, your organizational context, or your legal constraints. It can't tell you if a scenario is inappropriate for your audience or if a stat is out of date. You are the subject matter expert. You are the final editor.
2
What to review in AI-generated L&D content
Accuracy (facts, dates, stats). Alignment (does it achieve the learning objective?). Tone (brand voice, inclusion, accessibility). Completeness (is anything missing?). Attribution (did AI pull from outdated sources?)
3
The Curate → Embed cycle
Curation isn't a one-time step — it's iterative, like SAM. You review, you refine, you re-prompt if needed. Once you're satisfied, Embed: plug it into your workflow template, your LMS, your facilitation plan. That's when AI effort becomes a reusable organizational asset.
4
Mapping PACE to ADDIE / SAM
Purpose → Analysis & Design phases. Augment → Development. Curate → Review & Evaluation. Embed → Implementation. AI doesn't replace the model — it compresses the timeline within it.
Differentiated Support — POC Scope
🟢
Beginner Track — POC Goal

Your proof of concept = one refined asset:

  • Review your visual concept OR your written draft
  • Make 2–3 edits that make it feel like your work, not AI's
  • Be ready to share: "I used AI to [do X] and I changed [Y] because [Z]"

That's a complete, shareable proof of concept.

🔵
Intermediate Track — POC Goal

Your proof of concept = a paired asset set:

  • Combine your Module 1 visual + Module 2 written into one cohesive asset
  • Ensure they share the same topic, tone, and intended audience
  • Apply your PACE framework mapping — note which step each asset represents
  • Identify: where would this fit in your real workflow next week?

🏗 Proof of Concept Activity (12 min)

Bring your Module 1 and Module 2 work together. Apply your human review. Refine. Connect it to your real workflow.

STEP 1

Review your visual asset — edit anything that doesn't fit your context or voice

STEP 2

Review your written asset — check accuracy, alignment, and tone

STEP 3

Connect them: do both serve the same learning purpose?

STEP 4

Write one sentence: "With AI, I built ___. I can use this for ___ at work."

CoPilot → PowerPoint design brief

You are an instructional designer and visual design consultant. Create a design brief for a [ASSET TYPE] on the topic of [TOPIC]. The audience is [AUDIENCE DESCRIPTION]. The tone should be [TONE]. You are an instructional designer. Write a facilitator guide intro for a [DURATION]-minute session on [TOPIC] for [AUDIENCE]. Include: a welcome statement, session goals (3 bullets), a warm-up discussion question, and timing guidance. Tone: professional and encouraging.

🎙 Podcast Script Prompt

You are a podcast producer for a professional development series. Write a 90-second intro script for an episode titled "[EPISODE TITLE]." The host is [HOST DESCRIPTION]. The episode is for [AUDIENCE]. Start with an attention-grabbing hook. End with a preview of what listeners will learn. Tone: [TONE].

🎬 Video Script Prompt

You are a video scriptwriter for corporate learning. Write a [DURATION]-second explainer video script about [TOPIC] for [AUDIENCE]. Include: Scene 1 — hook/problem (15 sec), Scene 2 — key insight (30 sec), Scene 3 — application example (25 sec), Scene 4 — call to action (10 sec). Tone: [TONE].

🔍 Human Review Checklist

  • ✅ Factual accuracy verified against a trusted source
  • ✅ Aligns with stated learning objective(s)
  • ✅ Reflects organizational voice and brand
  • ✅ No PII, confidential, or inappropriate content
  • ✅ Accessible language (aim for 8th grade reading level)
  • ✅ Inclusive and culturally appropriate
  • ✅ Copyright-clear (no verbatim lifted text)
  • ✅ Meets your org's AI disclosure policy

Take-Home: The PACE Framework Card

P

Purpose — Define Before You Design

Clarify the learning need. Understand your audience. Apply AI governance principles. Ask: Is AI the right tool for this asset? What are the guardrails? Are you working within your organization's approved AI framework?

A

Augment — Prompt with Specificity

Use RTCF (Role, Task, Context, Format). What you put in is what you get out. Iterate 2–3 times. Use CoPilot for content, Microsoft 365 for design. AI accelerates — you direct.

C

Curate — Human Review Always

Review for accuracy, alignment, tone, and inclusion. Edit until it sounds like you. Run the Human Review Checklist. You are the author — AI is the collaborator.

E

Embed — Integrate into Your Workflow

Map PACE to your existing ADDIE/SAM process. Build reusable prompt templates. Document your workflow. Scale across your team. Make AI-assisted design your new standard.
